The Daily Graph¶

Introduction and summary.

Once again, I've re-organized things a bit. I dropped Suffolk and Hartford counties, since things have been rather constant there over the last 2-3 weeks. Not zero new cases, but an extremely small number based on the populations and past history of those counties.¶

I've dropped the doubling time analysis. With the increasing numbers of cases occurring in all of the counties, I think that analysis is redundant with just examining moving averages. (As the new cases go up, the doubling times go down.)¶

The spike Leon County continues and has actually gotten worse. We hit an all time high in the number of new cases reported on 7/3/2020, 228 for a single day. On 7/4/2020 there were 172 cases. I'm reasonably sure that some, but not all, of the spike is due to increased testing. Nonetheless, this appears to be a spike.¶

Although not as striking as Leon county, for both Hamilton and Fulton Counties number of new cases daily appear to be increasing rapidly.¶

The Campbell county data is the most difficult to interpret. The moving average number of new cases daily is increasing but no where near the level of the other counties. I suspect but have absolutely no evidence, that there is a reasonable probability that some cases for Campbell county are actually counted in Hamilton county since I think a reasonable number of people in Campbell county get their medical care in Hamilton county.¶

Log rate of occurrence
I left all counties on the same graph for now since the log scale compensates a bit for the rather large differences. Recall that if the occurrence was exponential, the semi-log graph would be linear. This doesn't occur in any of the counties. As the above graph demonstrated, after about 30 days, Hamilton and Fulton counties are extremely similar. Suffolk county has, at least almost, reached a plateau (meaning very few new cases).
