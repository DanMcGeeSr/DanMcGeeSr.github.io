Examine new cases, moving averages

The moving averages are normalized as the number of cases per 100,000 population. As noted before, the averages are 'trailing' in that the point on the graph represents the average number of new confirmed cases for that day and the $k-1$ previous days ($k = 5,7,10$).

Log rate of occurrence
I left all counties on the same graph for now since the log scale compensates a bit for the rather large differences. Recall that if the occurrence was exponential, the semi-log graph would be linear. This doesn't occur in any of the counties. As the above graph demonstrated, after about 30 days, Hamilton and Fulton counties are extremely similar. Suffolk county has, at least almost, reached a plateau (meaning very few new cases).
