Some doubling time analyses.

Recall that I earlier noted that if we have a model:
$\log(y)=\beta_0 + \beta_1 * t$ The time it takes to double the number of cases is $\frac{\log_{10}(2)}{\beta_1}$.
In the blog I cited, Wicklin used 5 days data to derive his estimates, as I recall the last 5. In this initial look I used three analyses of doubling time estimated using 5 , 7, and 10 days. The estimates were calculated for the k days including day 0, the k days including day 1, and so on until there were less than k days left. In each case a linear model was fit, and the doubling time estimated based on that model.
