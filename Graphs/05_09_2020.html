What's next?¶

There is (at least visually) two patterns of increase. I'd like to start exploring this a bit.

The ideas here orignated in a SAS blog that I subscribe to (and highly recommend).

https://blogs.sas.com/content/iml/2020/04/01/estimate-doubling-time-exponential-growth.html?utm_source=feedburner&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=Feed%3A+TheDoLoop+%28The+DO+Loop%29

A confession: I started with SAS, and things would have gone quicker if I'd have stayed with it. But I thought and still do that it was a good opportunity to increase my knowledge of python. So, things tend to go a bit slower.

The basic idea is that we assume exponential growth: at any time $t$ the cumulative cases ($y$) are:

$y=e^{\beta t}$
and $log(y) = \beta t$

This formalization allows us to approach the problem using a simple linear model approach. The question becomes is $\beta$ constant. If not, where are the changes, and at what point, if at all, does $\beta$ become essentially zero (flattening the curve).
